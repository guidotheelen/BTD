Meet Your Guide: Guido Theelen

Psychedelic space is vast, mysterious, and at times overwhelming. A guide does not direct the journey—but ensures that you are never lost.

Who I Am

My name is Guido Theelen. I walk with those who are ready to explore the hidden chambers of their consciousness—those who seek truth not in abstraction, but in direct experience.

I am both software developer and bushcraft practitioner. I work at the intersection of structure and surrender, logic and intuition. My life’s path has taught me to navigate complexity—technological, psychological, and spiritual.

Guiding psychedelic experiences is not a profession. It is a vocation. One born from direct contact with the transformative power of plant medicines and the responsibility they demand.

My Approach

Presence Over Prescription

I do not interpret your experience. I witness, I anchor, I reflect only when invited. The wisdom lies within you.

Safety Through Structure

Every journey is preceded by rigorous screening, preparation, and clarity. I hold the container firmly—so you can let go fully.

Integration as the Real Work

The trip is only the threshold. What you build with what you see—that’s the work we do together afterward.

Training and Experience

  • Years of direct experience with psilocybin, San Pedro, cannabis, and other plant teachers
  • Bushcraft and survival training: comfort with wilderness and natural ceremony
  • Workshops given on AI, human cognition, and self-reflection
  • Personal meditation and somatic disciplines
  • Ongoing study in the ethics and neuropsychology of altered states

Why I Guide

I guide because I’ve seen what happens when people are left to interpret the ineffable alone. I guide because transformation is not safe in the abstract—it requires space, stillness, and someone to hold the edges.

I do not claim to heal. I create the space where healing becomes possible.

What You Can Expect from Me

  • Calm, grounded presence
  • No spiritual bypassing or exaggerated claims
  • Clarity about boundaries and responsibilities
  • Non-judgmental space for whatever arises
  • Absolute confidentiality and discretion
"The role of a guide is not to show you the way—it is to remind you that you already carry the compass."
