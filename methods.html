How We Work: Rituals of Repair in the Theatre of the Mind

This is not therapy as you've known it. This is not medicine in the allopathic sense. This is initiation. The reactivation of ancient pathways in the psychic wilderness. To enter this work is to recognize that consciousness is the medium, and meaning is the medicine.

Psychedelics—when properly held in ritual, reverence, and preparation—are not substances. They are emissaries. Agents of the Other. They do not conform to reductionist frameworks or clinical neatness. They demand poetry. They demand myth. And they return what you bring: honesty, humility, curiosity.

1. Preparation: Tilling the Psychic Soil

No molecule will save you from yourself. The groundwork must be laid before the journey begins. This includes:

  • Intake & Readiness Assessment – Psychological safety and physiological screening. We do not open gates blindly.
  • Intention Setting – Not goal-setting. Not wishful thinking. A dialogue with the soul about why you’ve come.
  • Education – You will not be walking into the unknown unarmed. We provide maps—but no map is the territory.

We prepare the body. We soften the ego. We court the archetypes.

2. The Journey: A Theatre of Transformation

In the presence of these ancient molecular intelligences, the ordinary dissolves. Language fails. Time ruptures. What remains is encounter.

  • Set and Setting – Every journey unfolds within a carefully curated environment: natural, safe, and symbolically resonant.
  • Facilitator Presence – We are not interventionists. We are watchers of the flame. Witnesses. Guardians of liminality.
  • Sacrament – We work with ethically sourced, traditionally honored plant medicines. These are not 'drugs'—they are co-evolutionary partners.

And here, a reminder: plant DNA is vastly more complex than human DNA. The chlorophyll-based lifeforms we patronize as primitive contain genomic symphonies far beyond our own. What does this tell us about the true hierarchy of species? Who is the teacher, and who the student?

3. Integration: Anchoring the Myth

A psychedelic experience without integration is spiritual pornography. A flash without fire. We walk with you as the vision reenters language, relationship, habit, and choice.

  • One-on-One Integration Sessions – Conversation as alchemy. We assist in decoding what the symbols brought forth.
  • Community Integration Circles – Shared mythology. No one emerges alone.
  • Long-Term Tools – Practices, journaling, ritual suggestions. Not prescriptions, but invitations.

You will not be handed answers. You will be handed a mirror.

We do not claim mastery. We do not package the infinite. Our method is structured only to the extent that it protects what is unstructured. Mystery resists domestication.

The Plants We Work With

Different plants offer different lessons. Some open the heart. Some pierce illusions. Some ground the body in silence. We work only with plant-based psychedelics whose safety profiles, historical roots, and effects are well-understood.

1. Morning Glory (Ipomoea tricolor)

Active Compound: LSA (d-lysergic acid amide)

Effects: Dreamlike visions, introspection, emotional processing

Duration: 6–8 hours

Origin: Mesoamerican ritual use; associated with divination and communication with the spirit world

Use in Our Practice:
  • Ceremony-style sessions focused on symbolic insight and emotional clarity
  • Slower onset encourages meditative preparation and internal dialogue
Notes:
  • Strong nausea common during onset; physical stillness encouraged
  • Legal status varies—only seeds with no treatment used

2. Psilocybin Truffles (Sclerotia of Psilocybe mexicana/tampanensis)

Active Compound: Psilocybin (converted to psilocin in the body)

Effects: Ego dissolution, emotional catharsis, perceptual reorganization

Duration: 4–6 hours

Origin: Global indigenous use, prominently Mesoamerican

Use in Our Practice:
  • One-on-one or small-group settings with controlled sensory environment
  • Music, silence, and breathwork integrated
  • Ideal for inner work, trauma processing, or spiritual inquiry
Notes:
  • Legal in the Netherlands under specific circumstances
  • Dosage calibrated based on intent and experience level

3. San Pedro Cactus (Echinopsis pachanoi)

Active Compound: Mescaline

Effects: Open-hearted connection, expansive awareness, spiritual grounding

Duration: 10–14 hours

Origin: Andes Mountain region; used in ceremonial healing traditions for millennia

Use in Our Practice:
  • Conducted outdoors (weather permitting), aligned with solar rhythms
  • Integration walks, ritual sharing, and deep body grounding
Notes:
  • Long, slow experience suited for those with prior psychedelic exposure
  • Strong emotional resonance and sense of unity common

4. Marijuana (Cannabis sativa/indica, spiritual use only)

Active Compounds: THC, CBD, other cannabinoids

Effects: Emotional softening, body awareness, enhanced receptivity

Duration: 2–4 hours

Origin: Spiritual use across cultures (India, Africa, Rastafari, Middle East)

Use in Our Practice:
  • Only used in guided introspective sessions or pre-journey priming
  • Helps some participants with anxiety regulation or somatic access
Notes:
  • Not suitable for all; may exacerbate anxiety/paranoia in some profiles
  • Low-dose, ritual context only—not casual use

Safety, Legality, and Integrity

Every session is preceded by thorough screening and orientation. We do not offer substances directly—we facilitate legal, client-sourced sessions in permitted jurisdictions only. Nothing proceeds without full informed consent.

Plant work is sacred. Methods are selected based on the client’s intention, medical profile, and readiness—not preference or curiosity.

You don’t choose the plant—the plant calls when you’re ready.
